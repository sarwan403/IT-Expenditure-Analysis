# IT-Expenditure-Analysis
Corporate IT Expenditure Analysis Dashboard
📌 Project Overview
In large enterprise environments, corporate Information Technology (IT) departments are frequently perceived as financial "black boxes" with volatile, unpredictable spending patterns. This project delivers a professional business intelligence framework designed to bring absolute financial transparency, cost-driver tracking, and budget compliance across global corporate functions.

By establishing an end-to-end data transformation pipeline and interactive reporting architecture, this project translates multi-dimensional financial ledgers into actionable executive insights. The solution highlights areas of operational overspending, structural budget underutilization, and alignment variance between actual costs, rolling forecasts, and original capital expenditure plans across geographies, business units, and technical asset categories.

🎯 Key Analytical Objectives
Cost Distribution Mapping: Determine how IT capital and operating expenses flow across individual organizational functions (Business Area), global zones (Region), and underlying technical asset classes (IT Area).

Multi-Scenario Variance Analysis: Quantify and isolate tracking gaps between Actual vs. Forecast, Actual vs. Plan, and Forecast vs. Plan to gauge department-level forecasting accuracy.

Granular Strategic Drilldowns: Segment and isolate cost centers within specific sub-areas (e.g., separating internal labor allocations from external contractor spending and shared services distribution models).

Interactive Executive Enablement: Build an interactive presentation tier featuring intuitive cross-filtering matrices, high-level financial KPI cards, and trend visualizations for rapid business decision-making.
